# The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office
### A Survey-Based Analysis of Productivity, Health, and Work Preferences Among 1,000 Employees

## Overview

This project explores the impacts of working remotely versus working in an office environment. It includes a detailed research report, a 1,000-respondent survey dataset, and a full data analysis using Python. The research investigates productivity, mental and physical health, flexibility, social interaction, focus, and overall work preferences across different industries, age groups, and work arrangements.

## Features

- Survey dataset with 1,000 responses and 16 variables
- Comprehensive research report with embedded figures and tables
- Statistical analysis and cross-tabulations of survey responses
- Data visualizations for each survey question
- Insights and recommendations for hybrid work models

## Requirements

- Python 3.x
- Jupyter Notebook
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

## Usage

1. **Run the Jupyter Notebook**:
    ```bash
    jupyter notebook "The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office Survey code.ipynb"
    ```

2. **Follow the notebook**: Execute the cells in the notebook to perform data analysis and visualization.


## File Structure

- `The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office Survey code.ipynb`: Jupyter Notebook containing the full data analysis and visualizations.
- `The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office Survey.csv`: CSV file with 1,000 survey responses and 16 columns.
- `The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office.docx`: Word document containing the full research report with embedded figures and tables.
- `The Impacts of Working Remotely and in an Office.pdf`: PDF version of the research report.
- `Charts/`: Folder containing pre-exported chart images.

## Dataset Summary

The survey covers 10 questions across the following topics:

- Whether respondents have experienced working from home
- Productivity, flexibility, and time savings when working remotely
- Physical and mental health risks by work type
- Social isolation and focus levels
- Overall work type preference

**Key finding:** 48% of respondents prefer a mixed/hybrid model, 33% prefer fully in-office, and 19% prefer fully remote work.

## Example

Upon running the Jupyter Notebook, you will be able to see various visualizations and statistical analyses such as:

- Distribution of responses for each survey question
- Comparison of productivity between remote and office work

- Cross-tabulations by work arrangement, age group, and industry
